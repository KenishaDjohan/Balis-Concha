How to Make an Aromatic Scented Candle Out of Used Cooking Oil

Tools and Materials Needed:

  • Used cooking oil (that has already been filtered)
  • Citric Acid
  • Candle Wick
  • Glass jar or Mold
  • Essential Oil (lavender, vanilla or other scents)
  • A stick

Procedure:

  1. First, heat up the cooking oil that has been filtered through a strainer to remove any food particles in it.
  2. A glass jar or mold is prepared by placing the candle wick in the center. The top of the wick is held in place by a stick laid on the rim of the jar.
  3. The filtered oil is heated gently until it becomes warm, but not boiling. Once the oil is heated up, mix in the citric acid
  4. The warm oil is poured carefully into the jar, ensuring the wick stays centered.
  5. A few drops of essential oil are added to the warm oil for fragrance.
  6. The candle is left to cool and solidify for a few hours.
  7. Once solid, trim the wick to about 1 cm above the wax surface.

How to Make Bali Batik Ribbonette

Tools and Materials Needed:

  • Scissors (Paper / Cutter): Used to cut parchment paper.
  • Fabric Scissors: Used to easily cut batik fabric and thread.
  • Sewing Machine: Makes stitching the batik fabric easier.
  • Ruler: Used to measure the fabric size.
  • Pins: Used to secure the fabric and prevent it from shifting.
  • Sewing Needle: Used for sewing the fabric with thread.
  • Glue Gun: Used to attach clips to the ribbon.
  • Pencil: Used for marking the fabric size.
  • Bali Batik Fabric (Motif Pisang Bali, Barong Bali, Bali): Used as the base material for creating this art piece. 3m x 4m is needed for 6 ribbons (for 10 ribbons, 6.4m x 5m).
  • Pattern Paper / Parchment Paper: Used for cutting the fabric patterns.
  • Thread: Chosen to match the batik fabric, used to join fabric pieces together.
  • Clips / Automatic Pin: Used to hold the ribbon in place on the hair.

Procedure:

  1. Prepare all the tools and materials needed for making the batik ribbon.
  2. Draw the pattern on the fabric according to the required sizes (29x29 cm, 15x27 cm, and 6x8 cm), and the pattern is then cut.
  3. Pin the pattern onto the batik fabric, and the fabric is cut according to the pattern into several pieces with the specified sizes.
  4. Fold the 15 x 27 cm fabric piece in half, and the edges are stitched together, leaving one end unsewn.
  5. Afterward, turn the fabric inside out, so that the stitches are hidden.
  6. The remaining edges are sewn together.
  7. Take the 29 x 29 cm fabric piece and fold it into a triangle. The edges of the triangle are stitched using the sewing machine, leaving one end unsewn.
  8. Turn the fabric inside out so that the stitched part is hidden.
  9. Prepare the 6 x 8 cm fabric piece, fold it in half along its length, and one side of the width is sewn.
  10. Turn the fabric back to its original position, so that the initially hidden side is now on the outside.
  11. Take the previously stitched 15 x 27 cm fabric, fold it again in half, and use a needle and thread to stitch it together, creating the shape of a ribbon.
  12. Attach the 29 x 29 cm fabric piece and it is sewn to the ribbon, connecting the pieces.
  13. Take the last fabric piece of 6 x 8 cm, and it is sewn to the center of the ribbon, completing the bow shape.
  14. Finally, attach the clip to the back of the ribbon using the glue gun, and the Bali Batik Ribbon is complete.
